11.1 TIMES Attributes used in the Residential Sector#

Attribute

Description

Purpose in Model

Units

TechName

Name of the technology or appliance (e.g., Electric Heater).

Identifies the technology being modeled.

N/A

Comm-IN

Incoming commodities or inputs to the technology.

Represents the resources entering the technology.

N/A

Comm-OUT

Outgoing commodities or outputs from the technology.

Represents the products generated by the technology.

N/A

EFF

Efficiency of the technology in converting energy.

Indicates how effectively the technology utilizes energy.

Percentage (%)

STOCK

Stock of the technology available in the residential sector.

Reflects the total amount of existing installed capacity of the technology.

Petajoule-annum (PJa)

INVCOST

Investment cost associated with the technology.

Represents the capital investment required for the technology.

2005 mill. R/PJ-a

NCAP_BND~0

Capacity bound at the initial period (year 0).

Sets the initial capacity limits for the technology.

kW or MW

EFF~(year)

Efficiency of the technology for a given year.

Indicates the expected performance in that year.

Percentage (%)

FLO_MARK (UP)

Upper bound on the flow of energy between processes or from a process to a market.

Sets an upper constraint on how much energy (or material) can flow from a technology (e.g., solar heater) to a market.

Percentage (%)

FLO_MARK (LO)

Lower bound on the flow of energy between processes or from a process to a market.

Sets a minimum requirement for how much energy must flow to a market from a given technology (e.g., electric heaters).

Percentage (%)
